Origin


At the end of the 19th century, rowing dominated Rio de Janeiro. Football was just beginning to appear in some clubs, but it was still viewed with some fear, as it was not being received with enthusiasm by Rio society. The creation of an organized group with the aim of competing in rowing competitions with clubs from other neighborhoods emerged among young people from the Flamengo neighborhood, at Café Lamas, in Largo do Machado.


Nestor de Barros, José Agostinho Pereira da Cunha, Felisberto Laport, Augusto Lopes, Mário Spindola and José Félix da Cunha Meneses bought a boat, called it "Pherusa" and renovated it.


On October 6, 1895, those mentioned above, together with Maurício Rodrigues Pereira and Joaquim Bahia, left Ponta do Caju, and with the unfavorable weather, headed towards Praia do Flamengo, but the wind caused the boat to capsize. Bahia swam to the beach to get help and arrived a few hours later, but the rain stopped quickly and another boat, the "Leal", rescued the young people and what was left of Pherusa. Then a new renovation of the vessel began, but it was stolen and disappeared.


Foundation


A new boat was purchased and named "Scyra". On the night of November 17, 1895, many people were in one of the corridors of house number 22 in Praia do Flamengo, where Nestor de Barros lived in one of the rooms. There, a long time ago, they had already sheltered "Pherusa", and now they guarded "Scyra". The purpose of the meeting was to found the Flamengo Regatta Group. That same night the first board of directors was elected: Domingos Marques de Azevedo (president); Francisco Lucci Colas (vice-president); Nestor de Barros (secretary); Felisberto Cardoso Laport (treasurer)


The initial colors were blue and gold in very wide horizontal stripes, however, in 1898, at the proposal of Nestor de Barros, there was a change to the current ones: red and black.


New boats were being purchased and Mengo began to stand out in competitions. In the 1st Regatta of the Brazilian Nautical Championship, on June 5, 1898, she won her first victory, with "Irerê", a two-oared whaleboat. Previously, Flamengo had only achieved secondary placements and many second places, which even earned it the nickname "Bronze Club". In 1902, given its growth, it was transformed into Clube de Regatas do Flamengo.


A curious fact in the history of the Clube de Regatas do Flamengo is that its athletes had already taken the risk of playing football. On October 25, 1903, before the founding of Flamengo's football department, the Flamengo rowers met with their Botafogo sports colleagues to play a friendly match.


The beginning in football


From 1902 onwards, rowing began to share popular preference with football. Thus, Flamengo members also became members of Fluminense to follow the football, and those from the Laranjeiras club came to the Red-Negro region to follow the regattas. Alberto Borgerth represented the example well, as in the morning he rowed for Flamengo and in the afternoon he played for his club, Fluminense.


However, in 1911, there was a split in Fluminense and many tricolor players came to Rubro-negro, deciding in an assembly on November 8, 1911 to found a land sports department, with Alberto Borgerth as director. The fight between Oswaldo Gomes and many of Fluminense's first team players was the reason for the discord. Originally, it was thought of simply joining Botafogo, but as the black and white team, at the time, was Tricolor Carioca's great rival, the idea was soon discarded. They then considered the idea of ​​reinforcing the already established Paysandu, but it was also vetoed, since the club was made up exclusively of English people. Finally, Borgerth came up with the idea of ​​creating a football section at Flamengo. The proposal was approved and enshrined at the club's assembly, held on the 8th.


"The Most Beloved in Brazil"


In 1927, a contest promoted by Salutaris mineral water and Jornal do Brasil aimed to elect the "most beloved club in Brazil". The fan should write the name of their favorite team on the label of the water bottle, or on the coupon printed in the newspaper, and send it completed to the Jornal do Brasil headquarters, in Rio de Janeiro. The winner would take to their headquarters the portentous Taça Salutaris and the "title" of the most beloved club in Brazil. At the end of the count, Flamengo received 254,850 votes and won the dispute. Currently, the Taça Salutaris is displayed in a prominent place in the trophy room of the Clube de Regatas do Flamengo, flanked by the Copa Libertadores da América and the 1981 Intercontinental Cup.


Another factor that helped to popularize Flamengo's strength across the country was the Second World War. With Brazil's positioning as an ally of the United States, two high-reduction antennas were built by the Americans in the cities of Natal-RN and Belém-PA to pick up signals sent from enemy ships. However, the same antennas also allowed the transmission of games, via radio, to the North and Northeast of the country.


At the time, with Rio de Janeiro as the country's capital, the importance of what happened in Rio de Janeiro was very high. Furthermore, radio was the most used means of communication for news and, of course, sports broadcasting. In this way, the victorious red and black campaigns in the state championships in the early 1940s spread, helping to popularize the club.
