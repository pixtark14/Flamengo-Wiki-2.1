This is a dedicated to the Clube de Regatas do Flamengo.


The Clube de Regatas do Flamengo (better known simply as Flamengo, popularly by the nicknames Fla, Mengo and Mengão, and whose acronym is CRF) is a Brazilian multi-sport association based in the city of Rio de Janeiro, capital of the state of the same name. Founded in the Flamengo neighborhood for rowing competitions on November 17, 1895, it became one of the most successful and popular clubs in Brazilian sport, especially football. Its traditional colors are red and black and its biggest sporting rivals are Vasco da Gama, Fluminense and Botafogo.


Among its greatest glories in football, the achievements of the Intercontinental Cup (the only team from Rio to have won a world title recognized by FIFA) and the Copa Libertadores da América in 1981, 2019 and 2022 (the only team from Rio to have won three times in the competition, and one of seven in Brazil to have won it more than once), in addition to a Recopa Sul-Americana, a Copa Mercosul and a Copa de Ouro Nicolás Leoz, which gives him fourth place in the competition. ranking of international titles of Brazilian clubs. When it comes to the Copa Libertadores da América, Flamengo is the fourth most successful in the competition, in addition to being the club with the best performance considering only duels between Brazilian teams until 2019.


In relation to national achievements, Flamengo is, by court decision, and then by the CBF, officially the holder of seven Brazilian Championship titles (1980, 1982, 1983, 1992, 2009, 2019 and 2020), four Brazilian Cup titles , two Brazilian Super Cups and a Champions Cup. These fourteen achievements give the club second place in the ranking of national titles, behind only Palmeiras (18 achievements). Regarding regional and state titles, the club won a Rio-São Paulo Tournament, thirty-seven Carioca Championship titles and twenty-three Guanabara Cups, being the biggest winner of state competitions. Due to these results, the club has been, since 2017, the national leader of the Folha Ranking, which gives a score for titles and runners-up won by teams, currently being one of the Brazilian clubs with the most achievements in terms of total. of recognized titles of national and international scope, the third Brazilian club (tied with Cruzeiro) in terms of the sum of recognized titles of international scope, the second in national scope, and in state terms, is the club from the State of Rio de Janeiro with the highest number of official titles in football, considering titles of all scopes (international, national and regional/state). It has the highest number of achievements in the Brazilian Championship since 1971 (when the name National Club Championship was adopted), with 7 titles (tied with Corinthians). It is also the team with the most games in contention: 1443 (1971 to 2021). It is also, according to a survey carried out by ESPN Brasil, the first (and for now only) club in Brazil to have won all possible national and international titles, namely: national championship, cup and super cup, in addition to the two main continental tournaments, the continental super cup and the intercontinental tournament.


Among other achievements, Flamengo was elected the ninth biggest football club of the 20th Century in a survey carried out by FIFA. In 2019, Flamengo was voted the best team in South America and the 4th best in the world according to the ranking prepared by IFFHS. Flamengo is one of three clubs from the so-called G-12 that have never been relegated to the second division in the Brazilian Championship. Along with Santos, it is also one of the only clubs to have won the Copa Libertadores and the Brazilian Championship in the same season (this feat achieved in 2019). It is also, alongside Atlético Mineiro, Santa Cruz, Arsenal (England), and Dublin (Uruguay), one of only five clubs in the world to have beaten the Brazilian Football Team. Along with Botafogo, it holds the longest undefeated streak in Brazilian football with 52 matches in 1979 and was the first club in Brazil to reach the mark of a thousand games in the first division of the national championship, in a match held against Santos on July 27, 2009.


Flamengo is the most popular football club in Brazil, with an estimated fan base of 40.4 million fans spread across all regions of Brazil. According to a survey conducted by the sports marketing agency Gerardo Molina-Euroamerica, Flamengo is, in absolute numbers, the football club with the largest number of followers worldwide. Due to the strength of its fans, it is the Brazilian club that receives the most amounts from broadcasting rights. Since 2018, Flamengo has been considered the most valuable club in Brazil, becoming in 2019 the most valuable team in South America, in addition to being the 70th most valuable football team in the world, valued at more than 145.7 million of euros. A Fla-Flu holds the world record for attendance at matches between clubs: 194,603 spectators, in the final of the 1963 Campeonato Carioca, won by Flamengo after a goalless draw.


In addition to its prestige with football, Flamengo also stands out in other collective and individual sports, notably rowing, water polo and basketball. In the latter, it is one of the most traditional clubs in the country, with its men's basketball team having won forty-seven state titles, eight Brazilian titles, a South American Championship of Champions Clubs, a South American League, a League of the Americas, one Champions League Americas and two FIBA ​​Intercontinental Cups.
